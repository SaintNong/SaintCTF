What's a CTF?

If you are new to CTFs, then here's an explanation:

A capture the flag (CTF) is a cybersecurity competition, where teams compete to solve as many challenges as they can, as fast as possible.

Challenges range across several categories, such as crypto(graphy), reverse engineering, and web exploitation.

Wikipedia explains it better than I can here.

Where do I get started?

There are many ways to get started with a CTF — you'll get the most out of it just by trying!

Just searching for things will get you further than you think in a CTF, especially if it's an unfamiliar topic — your search engine is your friend.

Here are some tools to get you started:

Tools

  • CyberChef — The Swiss Army knife of cybersecurity tools
  • dCode — A great tool with solvers for many common ciphers
  • Ghidra — The industry standard tool for reverse engineering developed by the NSA (Yes, that NSA!)
  • Burp Suite — A great web penetration testing framework
